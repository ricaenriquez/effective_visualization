Design

Specification

The visualization is an explanatory graphic. It communicates a clear finding or relationship in the data. Design choices foster communication between the reader and the visualization.

Does Not Meet Specification
Reviewer Comments

I do think this is an explanatory graphic although it borders on exploratory. It does tell me Sunday morning is probably a good time to fly and Thursday/Friday evening are not the best times to fly. But I wasn't really sure what the visualization was trying to tell me until after I read the README file; the visualization contains a lot of information, so there are a lot of ways to interpret the visualization. Once I read the README file, I understood that the idea was to show the best times and worst times to fly.

I always look at the chart first before the README file. I thought the story was trying to show that arrival delays were greater than departure delays given the animation. The README file discussed a different story (best vs worst time to fly), so we need to find a way to make sure that everybody who reads the chart will get the same story. If this were a martini glass narrative, this current visualization would be a great end to the martini glass where the user gets to explore all of the data on his own.

Here are a few suggestions for tilting the visualization towards an explanatory story:

  • If the story is about when are the worst or best times to fly, then I would first change the chart title to reflect that. I would also highlight the bars with the best time to fly and worst time to fly with different colors or with a higher opacity so that it sticks out to the reader when those times are. For example best time to fly could be green and worst time could be red.
  • If the story is about comparing arrival and departure delay times, that could also be interesting. It seems arrival delays are almost always greater than departure times. A really easy way to visualize this would be with a line chart showing time on the x-axis and arrival delay on the y-axis. That way both arrival delays and departure delays would be on the same chart at the same time, which makes it easier to make an explanatory narrative. With the current animation showing arrival and departure delays on separate visualizations, it makes it hard to do a true comparison since I can't see both charts at the same time.
  • If the story is about comparing arrival and/or delay times by month or year, then line charts would also be a good option to show multiple lines on one chart. I can't tell if that would make an interesting story; if the data fluctuates randomly, then it's not too interesting. But if some months or years were clearly worse than others, there's a story in that (like delays go up in winter time).

I think this blog article will be particularly helpful although the way she makes an explanatory chart is not the only way to do it: http://www.storytellingwithdata.com/blog/2014/04/exploratory-vs-explanatory-analysis

There are probably other stories to tell, and other ways to encode those stories. The crux is to make sure it's clear from the encodings and blurbs what the story is. The explanatory story should be prepackaged so that the reader doesn't have to guess or interpret the data on his own.

Specification

The visualization includes interaction or animation. The interaction or animation may be simple, such as a hover, tooltip, or transition. Interaction or animation enhance understanding of the data.

Does Not Meet Specification
Reviewer Comments

I was a little bit confused by the animation. My instinct is to click on the "departing" button to change the visualization to the "departing" data. Instead, I have to click on "departing", wait for the animation to start, and then click on "departing" again to stop the animation. Since there's also a big lag as dimple figures out how to filter and aggregate, I would get confused as to what was supposed to be happening.

I suggest having a separate start animation and stop animation button. Clicking on departure or arrival should filter the data without starting an animation.
